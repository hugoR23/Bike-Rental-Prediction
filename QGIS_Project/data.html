Data was obtained from the Local Employment Dynamics (LED) Partnership of the US Census Bureau at the census block group level. The LED Partnership defines a job as a link between a worker and a firm at which the worker has been employed during the reference quarter and during the quarter prior to the reference quarter. The reference quarter is Quarter 2 (April-June) of the year of interest. This definition of "job" is sometimes called a "Beginning of Quarter" job because it is assumed that the worker was employed at that firm on the first day of the reference quarter.

Blocks were aggregated to census tracts and density was calculated by dividing the number of jobs in a census tract by the area in square miles.

Field Definitions

Tract2010: 2010 census tract ID numbers

Pop2010: Population based on the 2010 US Census

Area_smi: Area of the census tract in square miles

Jobs_cnt: Number of jobs

Jobs_psmi: Jobs per square mile

Income data was collected from the American Community Survey (ACS). Per capita income is calculated by summing all of the income within a census tract and dividing it by the number of residents. Median household income is calculated by determining the collective income of each household in a census tract and then determining the median household income for that census tract. Data are presented at the 2000 census tract level. See use limitations for information on use and interpretation of ACS data.

Field Definitions

Tract2000: 2000 census tract ID numbers

MedInc_d: Median household income

MedInc_moe: Median household income (Margin of Error)

pC_Inc_d: Per capita income

pC_inc_moe: Per capita income (Margin of Error)

Sources

US Census Bureau, 2005-2009 American Community Survey
